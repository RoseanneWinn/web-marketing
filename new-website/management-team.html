Izenda Management Team


Sanjay Bhatia, Founder and CEO

Sanjay Bhatia is a first generation American, inventor, entrepreneur and pioneer in decision science and interactive data visualization. Sanjay is founder and CEO of Izenda, the market leader in self-service reporting for Microsoft's ASP.NET platform. The company has been highlighted by Microsoft for its refreshingly simple approach to data analytics, is an ATDC Graduate and has been named one of the most innovative companies in Georgia by TAG. TechAmerica nominated Izenda "Cool Technology" of the Year in 2010. Sanjay has also won TIE Entrepreneur of the Year Award as well as The Apex Society's Power 30 Under 30.

Sanjay has an MBA from Georgia State and graduated with high honors from Georgia Tech with a BS in Computer Science. Prior to founding Izenda, Sanjay worked in engineering and consulting positions at Radiant Systems, Trilogy and Microsoft.

Sanjay has been highlighted on Sky Radio, Channel 9, Jezabel, Micro-ISV, The Startup Podcast and Atlanta Business Radio X.

Sanjay enjoys traveling to the world's major cities, power yoga, meditation, hiking, biking, cooking, HDR Photography, acrylic painting and mixed medium digital artwork.

His vision and mission is to create simple tools that help people better understand their information in order to enhance the quality of their lives and organizations.


Stephen J. Walden, CFO

Steve Walden is a marketing-savvy senior executive, a successful entrepreneur and a private-equity investor. His chief passion is building businesses from scratch to multimillion dollar levels.

As Executive Director of the IBM/Sears joint venture called Prodigy, he helped launch the first consumer online service and grew it to a revenue level exceeding $100 million. This pioneering effort required the invention of new marketing techniques which are commonplace today such as introducing software both to the OEM and to retail channels. At one point his software became the largest selling brand in the retail market.

At BellSouth (now AT&T) he was a Vice President in the online service division, where his team launched not only BellSouth.net but also several other related businesses, including a webhosting business that grew to several hundred million dollars in value.

Later, he took over the role of Chief Executive Officer of a failing technology company and led it to a highly successful turnaround. He completely revitalized the business, leading it into new markets with new products and new energy. Shareholders received major cash payouts as a result of this success.

Earlier in his career, as a senior vice president at Grey Direct in New York (a subsidiary of Grey Advertising), he learned how to grow and manage profitably a big-ticket fee-for-service business. The agency billed in excess of $120 million, entirely through fee-generating projects and relationships. Currently, he serves on the Boards of multiple companies and nonprofit organizations and, as an angel investor, analyzes market opportunities, evaluates management teams and risks his own money on companies with growth potential.
